World Leader

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez

American politician

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez became the youngest women to serve in the US Congress, at age 29, on January 3, 2019 when she was sworn into office. Ocasio-Cortez was born in Brox, New York and later moved to Yorktown Heights when she was five. As she grew up in her Puerto Rican family and visited family in Brox, she saw the difference in opportunities. She attend Yorktown High School and then later Boston University. Interning for Senator Ted Kenndy during college gave her exposure to the effects of ICE which led her to eventually working as the Education Director at the nonprofit National Hispanic Institute. After college, Ocasio-Cortez moved back to Brox, working 18 hour bartending shifts to help her mother keep their house after her father passed away from lung cancer. Later in 2016, Ocasio-Cortez became a volunteer organizer for Bernie Sanders presidential campaign. Afterwards, she traveled across the country to support indigenous communities impacted by the Flint Water Crisis. This experience would later inspire her to serve her community and eventually run for congress.

Athlete

Naomi Osaka

Professional Tennis player

Naomi Osaka was born in Osaka, Japan on October 16, 1997 to her Japanese mother and Haitian father. When she was three, her family moved to New York to later move to Florida when she was eight for more tennis training opportunities. Her father was inspired by the Williams Sisters success story and was determined to emulate the same training for his two daughters. Even though she lives in the US, her parents decided she would represent Japan because it was her birth country. By 2013, Osaka started playing professionally and then for the next few years rose through the ranks. In 2016, she received the Woman of Tennis Newcomer Award after reaching the top 50 of the WTA rankings. In 2018, at the Indian Wells Open won her first WTA title. This was then followed by her winning the US Open in September by defeated her childhood ideal, Serena Williams. This made her the first Japanese player to win a major singles tennis tournament. Most recently, Osaka has won the Australian Open. Although she has an aggressive playing style on the court, Osaka actually is known for her shy personality and dry humor.

Amy Yin

Software engineer

Amy Yin is currently a software engineer at Coinbase Commerce, a digital currency exchange company in San Francisco. Yin graduated from Harvard with a bachelor of computer science. She co founded Harvard Women in Computer Science where she raised money to send girls to the Grace Hopper Convention, a conference best known for networking and recruiting for computing girls. She is also a volunteer instructor for Girls Who Code at a local public library in California.

Medicine

Najaha Musse

Doctor

Born in rural Oromia, Ethiopia, Najaha Musse moved to the US at the age of 7 as a refugee. Growing up, Musse had to act as the interpreter for her parents when visiting the doctor. She was the first of her family to graduate from high school. She went to St. Olaf College in Minnesota where she had many experiences to travel while learning. Musse pursues Osteopathic Medicine, allowing her to have a licence to practice medicine in all states and other various countries. Musse believes the Osteopathic approach will help alleviate issue of the intersection of chronic illnesses and marginalized communities. Most recently, Musse has had the opportunity through the EqualHealth organization to attend a social medicine course in Haiti. Here, she could experience the socioeconomic situation of Haiti while studying it.
