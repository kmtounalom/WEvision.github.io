Mae C. Jemison

Physician and former NASA astronaut

Mae C. Jemison is best known as the first African American women to travel in space for her time aboard the Endeavor Space Shuttle. Born in Alabama and raised in Chicago, Jemison’s parents supported her interests in the sciences. Growing up, Jemison loved danced and studying nature. Jemison’s desire to be an astronaut was heavily inspired by Lieutenant Uhura on Star Trek, played by Nichelle Nichol, a popular African American actress. In pursuit of being an astronaut, Jemison earned her degrees of chemical engineering at Stanford and a medical degree at Cornell. For two years with the Peace Corps, Jemison was a doctor in Liberia and Sierra Leone. After a few years upon returning to the US, Jemison applied for NASA.
